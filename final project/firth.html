Firth, Idaho

A Brief History

Firth was started as a Swedish settlement in 1885. It was named after Lorenzo J. Firth, an English emigrant. He gave land for the railroad section house and water tank; the railroad named the station for him in 1903. The post office was established in 1905. The city itself is quite small, having a population close to 500. The surounding rural area is quite large and active with farmers and smaller organizations of friendly neighbors.
